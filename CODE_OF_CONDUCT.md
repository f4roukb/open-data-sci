# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want OpenDataSci to be a project where anyone who shows up to contribute, report a bug, or ask a question feels welcome and respected — regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act in ways that build an open, welcoming, and healthy community.

## Standards

**Behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Being kind and constructive in feedback, even when disagreeing.
- Welcoming people of all experience levels, including beginners.
- Accepting that others may have different approaches and that discussion is how we converge on the right one.
- Giving credit where it's due.
- Focusing criticism on ideas and code, not on people.

**Behaviour that is not acceptable:**

- Harassment in any form — public or private.
- Sexualised language, imagery, or unwanted advances.
- Trolling, personal attacks, or deliberate provocation.
- Publishing others' private information (doxxing) without consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, or reviews.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour can be reported to the maintainers at b4farouk@gmail.com. All reports will be handled with discretion. Maintainers are obligated to maintain confidentiality regarding the reporter.

Depending on severity and pattern, responses may range from a private warning to a permanent ban from the project's spaces.

Maintainers who fail to enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face consequences determined by other members of the project's leadership.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other forum linked from this repository — as well as in public spaces when someone is representing the project.
